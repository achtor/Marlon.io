Exploring UCPD data.

Marlon.io (named lovingly after Plenar.io and Marlon C. Lynch) is intended to provide an easy interface and API for accessing and visualizing data from police records provided by the Unversity of Chicago Police Department (UCPD).

Background

The UCPD is a full-fledged police service in the service of the University of Chicago, and one of the largest private security forces in the world. It functions as a regular police force in the vicinity of the university, patrolling from 37th to 64th Street and from Cottage Grove to Lake Shore Drive.

While the UCPD has long provided periodically-updated logs of incident reports for each day, for most of its existence this was the extent of its publicly available data: as a private police force, it is not subject to the same constraints under the Freedom of Information Act as a public department. In response to petitioning for this information by community and student groups, including the Campaign for Equitable Policing, the UCPD announced it would begin releasing additional data on its website in June 2015.

The original incidents dataset, field interview records, and traffic stop information are the three datasets regularly updated by the UCPD, available on its Data and Information portal. These backlogs are in a somewhat awkward format for searching or compiling, so the aim of Marlon.io is to provide a simpler means of doing so. Refer to the API page for details on how to access the raw data, and the Explore page for visualizations and other analyses of these datasets created using our tool.

Other resources

Other UCPD resources

All of these are linked on the UCPD Data and Information portal, as mentioned above, but are compiled here for completeness' sake.
  • Illinois Traffic Stop Study - not UCPD-specific, but a statewise effort to analyze racial bias in traffic stops. Includes analyses of UCPD records.
  • UCPD Complaints process, including documents containing annual reports on reviewed complaints, as well as findings of the Independent Review Committee intended to act as an oversight on the process.
  • Crime Trends report by the UCPD.
  • Violent Crimes Report by the UCPD.
  • Arrest records are available under request; UCPD contact information is available here.

Other civic data in Chicago

  • The Citizens Police Data Project, a project of the Invisible Institute, hosts and presents data on complaints against CPD officers.
  • Plenar.io is a "centralized hub" for a great deal of Chicago data, including an API providing many different kinds of access and a flexible platform.
  • The Chicago Data Portal is the city's own repository for its datasets, from which many of the Plenar.io datasets are drawn.
  • Chi Hack Night is a weekly civic hacking meetup downtown, hosted by Open City Apps. DataMade is an associated civic tech company.
